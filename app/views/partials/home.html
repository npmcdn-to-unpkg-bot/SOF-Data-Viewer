The Sound of Fractions (SoF) is a new way to approach teaching fractions to students. We use music and rhythm to capture students' interest and challenge mathematical questions to them that they can answer musically using rhythms.

SoF utilizes several key areas to help students begin to understand the system of fractions:

Music
  • is embodied
  • can be entrained
Math
  • is ordered
  • is scalar
When we use music to interact with numbers and fractions we can ask important challenging questions like:
  • Which comes first? A the second beat in a rhythm with 3 beats or the second beat in a rhythm with 4 beats?
  • Who plays more? Someone who plays one half of a rhythm with 6 beats, or someone who plays one third of a rhythm with 10 beats?
